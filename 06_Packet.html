CASE-02 — Board-ready evidence packet

Structured, verified material for the hemato-oncology board. Not a diagnosis, not a prescription — the differential below is a list of questions, discordances and gaps for the clinician, built from 31 source documents and checked against PubMed.

Presentation

Female, ~44y (obesity, endometriosis; fragmented cross-border care). Fourteen-month course of progressive lymphadenopathy from a painful left cervical node (Aug 2025) to generalized bilateral adenopathy with splenomegaly (Jun 2026).

The central problem

Four pathology reads disagree: core (non-diagnostic) → whole-node IHC "reactive, sarcoid-like, not lymphoma" → slide review confirms reactive → paraffin re-review reverses to "possible mixed-cellularity Hodgkin lymphoma with a sarcoid-like reaction". Meanwhile the imaging progressed monotonically throughout (NodeRADS 4; splenic index 504) — a trajectory a benign reactive process does not follow.

What the system flags for the clinician (questions, not conclusions)

  • Are the CD30+/MUM1+/CD15−/EBV− large cells Reed-Sternberg cells or reactive immunoblasts? This single point flips the leading diagnosis.
  • The confirmatory IHC panel (CD30/CD15/PAX5/MUM1/fascin + EBER) recommended in June is not yet resulted — it is the discriminating step.
  • A negative FNA flow cytometry does not exclude Hodgkin — Reed-Sternberg cells are not captured by conventional flow.
  • Castleman disease is not excluded — HHV-8 and Castleman-targeted histology were advised but not done.
  • LDH is missing from every panel; there is no PET-CT or marrow — all needed the moment lymphoma is confirmed.

Discriminating step

Data gaps (surfaced, not hidden)

What this packet does NOT do

No diagnosis, no treatment, no final interpretation. The differential is a set of questions, discordances and gaps for the clinician, cross-checked with PubMed.
