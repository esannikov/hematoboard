Why a graph here — and only here

Most patient data is state, not a network — and state belongs in columns, not "balls and lines" (see State). But a differential is a genuine network: one finding bears on several diagnoses, and each diagnosis rests on several findings. This is that bipartite network. Green = a finding supports a hypothesis · red = argues against · grey = neutral / does-not-discriminate. Click any node to trace it. The two ringed findings are the linchpin: the confirmatory IHC resolves the whole graph. (Pattern precedent: VISTA Architect's graph-DB tumour-board UI; research doc §5.)

A reasoning aid, not a probability model — edges encode the direction of evidence stated in the documents, not a computed likelihood. MedAI Workbench · not a diagnosis
